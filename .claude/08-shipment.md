## 7 Term you must know

Here are the 7 essential terms and concepts you need to know before fully setting up Shiprocket, explained in detail:

### 1. Confirmation Calling

For Cash on Delivery (COD) orders, it is crucial to verify if the customer genuinely wants the product, as some orders might be fake or placed jokingly. While you can directly ship orders that have high-quality, detailed addresses (with proper building numbers, landmarks, and 10-digit mobile numbers), you must call the remaining customers to confirm their order. During this call, you confirm the product details and order value. If the customer agrees, you ship the order; if they decline, you cancel it.

### 2. Invoices, Labels, and Manifests

When you ship an order, specific documents must be generated:

- **Invoice:** This is the billing receipt placed inside the package for the customer, detailing the product purchased and its price.
- **Label:** This is the printout pasted on the outside of the packed order. It contains the shipping address, order number, tracking barcode, and instructions for the pickup boy.
- **Manifest:** This is a comprehensive list/document of all the orders scheduled for pickup on a given day (e.g., a list of 100 orders). When the assigned pickup boy collects the parcels from the supplier, he signs the manifest to confirm he received the exact number of packages. This acts as a vital proof of handover and builds trust between the supplier and the delivery company. These are downloaded as PDFs and sent to your supplier.

### 3. Pickup of Orders

Once your orders are packed, and the labels and manifests are generated and shared with the supplier, a courier assigned by the shipping platform travels to the supplier's location (e.g., a warehouse in Delhi). The physical act of the courier boy collecting all the packed orders from that location is referred to as the "pickup".

### 4. NDR (Non-Delivery Report) Calling

When an order is out for delivery, it may fail to reach the customer due to various reasons, such as the customer refusing the parcel, being unavailable, or the delivery boy making a fake attempt. Every shipped order typically gets three delivery attempts before it is sent back (NDR Attempt 1, 2, and 3). **NDR Calling** is the process where your team calls the customer to find out why the delivery failed, confirm their future availability, and request a re-attempt for the delivery. These calls are often recorded and uploaded to the Shiprocket panel.

### 5. RTO (Return to Origin)

If an order remains undelivered even after all three NDR delivery attempts (because the customer refused it, didn't pay the COD amount, or was continuously unavailable), the courier company sends the package back to your or your supplier's original location. These undelivered, returned orders are called RTO orders.

### 6. Return

Many people confuse "Return" with "RTO", but they are different. A **Return** happens _after_ an order has been successfully delivered to the customer. If the customer opens the package and finds the product broken, defective, or unsatisfactory, they may contact you for a refund or an exchange. Processing this request after a successful delivery is called a Return.

### 7. Shipping Calculations

Shipping rates are determined by the weight and dimensions of the packaged product. Courier companies have specific weight slabs (e.g., 0 to 500 grams, 500 grams to 1 kg) with fixed pricing for each slab.

- **Volumetric vs. Actual Weight:** After a product is packed, two weights are calculated: the actual physical weight and the volumetric weight (calculated using a formula based on the package's length, width, and height). The courier company will charge you based on whichever weight is **higher**.
- **Costing:** If an order is successfully delivered, you are charged the forward shipping fee plus the Cash on Delivery (COD) processing fee. However, if an order becomes an RTO (undelivered), you are not charged the COD fee, but you will have to pay the forward shipping fee _plus_ a return shipping fee to get the product back to your origin.
